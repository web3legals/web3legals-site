OECD Crypto Reporting Framework 2027: How Indian Web3 Businesses Must Prepare Now

By Rahul Pareek · 2026-06-21T22:56 · 8 min read

Introduction

A seismic shift in global crypto transparency is coming — and for Indian Web3 businesses, it is arriving earlier than most founders realise.

The OECD's Crypto-Asset Reporting Framework (CARF) — the global standard for automatic exchange of crypto tax information between governments — takes effect globally in 2027. India has committed to early adoption, with transaction-level reporting obligations already introduced from April 2026 under Budget 2025.

What this means in plain terms: the era of crypto anonymity is over. Every significant transaction, every account, every wallet linked to a reportable crypto business will be visible to tax authorities across participating jurisdictions.

What Is the OECD Crypto-Asset Reporting Framework (CARF)?

The CARF is a global tax transparency standard developed by the OECD. It requires Crypto-Asset Service Providers (CASPs) to collect and report detailed information about their customers' crypto transactions to tax authorities — who then automatically exchange this information with tax authorities in the customers' home countries.

Think of it as the crypto equivalent of the Common Reporting Standard (CRS) that already governs automatic exchange of financial account information between countries globally.

What Information Must Be Reported?

  • Full name, address, date of birth, and tax identification number of each customer
  • The type and amount of each crypto-asset transaction
  • The gross proceeds from disposals of crypto assets
  • The fair market value of crypto assets transferred to or from external wallets
  • Whether transfers go to or from unhosted wallets

India's Early Adoption: What Budget 2025 Changed

India's Budget 2025 introduced transaction-level reporting obligations for crypto assets from April 2026 — placing India ahead of the global CARF implementation timeline.

From April 2026:

  • All FIU-IND registered VDA service providers must maintain transaction-level records in CARF-compatible formats
  • Cross-border transaction reporting has been enhanced — transfers involving foreign counterparties require additional documentation
  • The 1% TDS regime continues with enhanced audit trails required for all deductions
  • OECD CARF compatibility is the stated direction — build your compliance systems to meet 2027 global standards now

Who Is Affected

Under CARF, a Crypto-Asset Service Provider includes:

  • Centralised and decentralised crypto exchanges
  • Crypto broker-dealers and payment processors
  • Platforms facilitating crypto-to-fiat conversion
  • NFT marketplaces where financial transactions occur above thresholds
  • Wallet providers with custody functions
  • DeFi protocols with identifiable admin key holders

Practical Impact on Indian Web3 Businesses

Customer Data Collection

Your KYC processes must collect all CARF-required data fields — full tax identification numbers, addresses, and date of birth for every reportable customer. Most basic KYC systems do not capture this level of detail.

Transaction Monitoring Upgrade

Your system must capture: asset type, quantity, timestamp, counterparty wallet address, and whether the counterparty is a hosted or unhosted wallet.

Unhosted Wallet Risk

If your users regularly transfer assets to self-custody wallets, you need a documented policy for handling these transfers — including enhanced due diligence for large unhosted wallet transfers.

Step-by-Step Preparation Checklist

Immediate — Do Now:
  • Audit your current KYC data against CARF required fields
  • Review your transaction monitoring system
  • Register with FIU-IND if not already done
  • Appoint a compliance officer
Next 3 Months:
  • Upgrade your KYC/AML system to CARF-compatible standards
  • Implement unhosted wallet identification procedures
  • Get a legal opinion on your specific CARF classification
Before April 2027:
  • Build or integrate CARF reporting infrastructure
  • Test your reporting system with sample data
  • Train your team on CARF obligations

The Opportunity in Compliance

CARF compliance is not just a burden — it is a competitive advantage. Institutional investors, banking partners, and enterprise customers are increasingly selecting platforms based on compliance credentials. A CARF-compliant platform signals institutional-grade operations.

Conclusion

India's early adoption path means CARF obligations are not a future concern — they are a current requirement. The businesses that build CARF-compatible compliance infrastructure now will thrive in the post-2027 global crypto landscape.
